```text
Primary ambition: Build an AI-assisted solution for the department
```


## 4. Department-level pain points

For a department of 80-100 people, the likely pain points are not only technical. They are operating-model issues.

### 4.1 Upstream delivery effort

```text
RD / QA / Meeting Notes
  -> RCD
  -> BD Mapping
  -> BD Draft
```

These steps require significant reading, structuring, interpretation, and manual documentation.

### 4.2 Senior resource dependency

```text
- Junior members may struggle to create high-quality BD artifacts.
- Senior members spend significant time reviewing and correcting outputs.
- Business and design knowledge often remains in people's heads.
- Delivery quality depends on reviewer availability.
```

### 4.3 Documentation and Excel burden

```text
- Basic Design artifacts are often Excel-heavy.
- Manual copying, formatting, mapping, and review preparation consume effort.
- Traceability between requirement sources and design outputs is difficult to maintain manually.
```

### 4.4 Traceability and governance need

```text
- Client-facing delivery requires evidence.
- AI-generated outputs must be source-grounded.
- Human gates are required before output becomes delivery artifact.
- Unsupported AI claims must be flagged.
```

---

## 5. Transformation opportunity

The department can create high leverage by starting with one governed AI-assisted delivery workflow.

Initial opportunity:

```text
RD / QA / Meeting Notes
  -> Requirement fact extraction
  -> RCD generation
  -> Human review
  -> BD mapping
  -> BD draft
  -> Validation
  -> Excel delivery pack
```

Business value levers:

```text
1. Reduce manual effort in upstream design work.
2. Improve review readiness.
3. Reduce inconsistency and rework.
4. Increase traceability coverage.
5. Create reusable delivery skills and workflow templates.
6. Build department-level AI capability that can scale beyond one project.
```

## 7. Important constraints

```text
1. AI should not replace BA or Architect decisions.
2. AI output should be treated as draft until human-approved.
3. Source reference is mandatory for critical output.
4. MVP should be narrow and measurable.
5. Full UI should not be built before proving workflow value.
6. The first use case must be realistic for the department's actual delivery work.
```
